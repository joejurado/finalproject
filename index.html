Worldwide Forest Cover

State of the Forests

Current forest cover worldwide totals to about 4 billion hectacres, but depletion rates have been increasing. In 2016, almost 30 million hectacres were lost. At that rate, it would be a little over 100 years before forests worldwide were lost entirely. The largest reason for such rapid deforestation is tropical rainforest being cleared for agriculture.
